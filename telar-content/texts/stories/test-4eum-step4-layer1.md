The sound with breath only (on the spout) would have created a higher-pitched whistle. With added water, the tone of the whistle becomes softer and may include a bubbling sound if the water is raised far above the bridge level. So the makeup (build) of a whistle vessel would change the tone of the sound even if two vessels used the same amount of water, and the same amount of air being blown in. Vessels usually contained depictions of beings for whatever ceremony was being performed (funeral, ritual, etc…). As such, these were used during those rituals as musical instruments, and the symbols designed would be something for the deceased to have in the tomb.
